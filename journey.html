Align the incentives and the frontier opens

The structural problem: the talent pipeline for frontier AI infrastructure sits in academia — physics, materials science, electrical engineering, computer engineering, software engineering — but academic labs lack the funding and hardware access the work demands. Large companies have the funding, the hardware, and the real problems, but their incentives point at quarterly delivery, not open education or broad talent development. Capability wasted on both sides.

The bet: align the incentives instead of asking for charity. Companies publish bounded real problems and fund the learning loop; students get the resources, mentorship, and hardware to learn by doing; verified results flow back as reproduced benchmarks, workload traces, open-source contributions, and proven hires. The loop pays for itself — every party's incentive points the same direction.

The lane is the whole stack: inference infrastructure runs from DRAM cell physics through packaging, kernels, schedulers, and cost models — exactly the path the continuous lab already walks. And learning stays fun on purpose: visible systems, fast feedback, real hardware, and receipts you can show.

Academia has the students. Industry has the funding and the problems. This product is the incentive alignment between them.

Bounded to open-ended, L1 to L9

The frozen retry task covers L1–L5 today. The take-home loop plus a real open-source issue is the designed L6 on-ramp. First marketplace entry: challenge/CHALLENGE.md, written in the full template every future company-, lab-, or maintainer-sourced challenge will use.

L1 · ReproduceReproduce a known result. npm test on the pinned baseline.
L2 · ExplainExplain why the result occurs. Context-lifetime classification.
L3 · ModifyChange one variable, measure the effect. One policy per run.
L4 · DiagnoseFind the cause of an unknown failure. Reproducible bad baseline.
L5 · ImproveProduce a verified improvement. −42% / −70% with tests passing.
L6 · ContributeUseful contribution to a real project. Take-home → vLLM / SGLang / LMCache on-ramp.
L7 · ExtendExtend existing research or infrastructure.
L8 · DiscoverNew result, failure mode, optimization, or design.
L9 · LeadDefine the problem, coordinate a team, review evidence, deliver.

Safety and ethics boundary. Company challenges never expose customer data, secrets, credentials, export-controlled information, sensitive infrastructure details, unreviewed proprietary code, or personal data — sanitized workloads, synthetic data, isolated environments, approved snapshots only. Not a cheap-labor marketplace: no unpaid speculative work that replaces employees; payment ties to clear scope, evidence, and review.

Final principle: the product should not merely teach students about advanced AI systems. It should help them become people who can improve those systems — and hand them the evidence that proves it.
